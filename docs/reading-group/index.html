Platonic Representation of Foundation Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials

Wednesday, June 10th, 2026 · 08:30 AM PT / 11:30 AM ET / 5:30 PM CEST

Add to calendar Join meeting
Foundation machine learning interatomic potentials (MLIPs) have emerged as powerful tools for atomistic simulation, yet different models encode chemical environments in incompatible latent spaces, limiting direct comparison and interoperability. The platonic representation hypothesis suggests that sufficiently capable models converge towards a shared statistical representation of reality. Will this hold for MLIPs and what materials insights we can get from their representations? In this talk, Dr. Zhenzhu Li will share their recent story trying to answer these questions.
